Adding Multiples of 10 and 100

What Are Multiples of 10?

Multiples of 10 are numbers like 10, 20, 30, 40, and so on. They are easy to recognize because they always end with a zero (0).

Understanding Place Value:

  • In any number, the tens place tells us how many groups of ten there are, and the ones place tells us the leftover single digits.
  • When adding multiples of 10, only the tens place changes, while the ones place stays the same.

Example : 47 + 30

Start with 47 (4 tens and 7 ones).
Add 30 (3 tens and 0 ones).
The tens become 4 + 3 = 7, and the ones stay 7 as 7 + 0 = 7.



Therefore, the answer is 77 (7 tens and 7 ones).


What Are Multiples of 100?

Multiples of 100 are numbers like 100, 200, 300, and so on. They are easy to recognize because they always end in two zeroes (00).


Understanding Place Value: In a three-digit number like 356, the digits represent 3 represents hundreds, 5 represents tens, and 6 represents ones.

When you add a multiple of 100, only the hundreds place changes because the tens and ones stay the same.

Three-digit number: 356 + 300

Add 300 (3 hundreds).


The hundreds become as 3 + 3 = 6


The tens become 5 as 5 + 0 = 5


The ones become 6 as 6 + 0 = 6



Therefore, the answer is 656 (6 hundreds, 5 tens, and 6 ones).
